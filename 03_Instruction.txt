Project 7 — Integrative Industry Synthesis
Applied Industry System Design, Ethical Reasoning, and Professional Defense

What You Will Submit
An industry-specific AI solution, system, or design artifact (for example: integrated notebook, workflow, prototype, case study, or technical design document)
A 1,500–2,000 word Reflective Synthesis Paper (PDF)
Any supporting notebooks, scripts, diagrams, or configuration files required to understand or run the system
A final mentor presentation and live defense (15 minutes)
Project Instructions
Task 1 — Select an Industry and Define the Problem Space
Choose a real-world industry or sector. Examples include:

Healthcare
Education
Finance
Manufacturing
Creative industries
Public policy
Climate or sustainability
Clearly define:

The industry context and problem being addressed
Why the problem is appropriate for AI-based solutions
The constraints, risks, or ethical considerations relevant to the industry
This project must go beyond a single technique and focus on applied system design.

Task 2 — Define the Integration Plan
Identify at least three prior capstone projects whose methods, ideas, or artifacts will be integrated. These may include:

Data and statistical reasoning
Machine learning models
Deep learning systems
Generative AI components
Agentic AI workflows
Clearly explain:

Which prior projects are being integrated
What each contributes to the final system
How the components interact or build on one another
This integration plan should guide the rest of the project.

Task 3 — Design the Integrated AI System or Framework
Design a cohesive system, workflow, or case study that integrates the selected components.

Your design must include:

A clear system overview or architecture description
Data flow, model flow, or agent orchestration logic
Assumptions and design tradeoffs
Boundaries of system capability and responsibility
An architecture diagram or structured system description must be included.

Task 4 — Implement or Construct the Applied Artifact
Create a new, integrative artifact that demonstrates the system design. This may include:

An integrated notebook or pipeline
A prototype workflow combining models, agents, or generative components
A detailed applied case study with technical artifacts
A system design document supported by executable components
The artifact must represent new work and not a simple resubmission of earlier projects.

Task 5 — Evaluate System Behavior and Outcomes
Evaluate how the integrated system behaves in realistic scenarios.

Your evaluation should address:

Whether the system meets its intended goals
Strengths and limitations of the integrated approach
Failure cases, risks, or unexpected behavior
Tradeoffs between performance, complexity, and responsible use
Evaluation may be qualitative, quantitative, or mixed, depending on the system.

Task 6 — Write the Reflective Synthesis Paper
Write a 1,500–2,000 word paper that synthesizes your work across the capstone.

Your paper must include the following sections:

Overview and Industry Context
Describe the industry problem and why it matters.

Integration Rationale
Explain why the selected AI methods were combined and how prior projects informed your design.

System Design and Technical Decisions
Describe the system architecture, workflows, and key technical choices.

Ethical Considerations and Responsible AI
Analyze ethical risks, bias, transparency, accountability, and safeguards specific to the industry and system.

Evaluation and Reflection
Discuss what worked, what did not, and what you learned from integrating across domains.

Professional Relevance
Explain how this work demonstrates readiness for real-world AI roles.

References
Include all cited sources using a consistent citation style.

Citations are required when referencing technical concepts, ethical frameworks, or best practices. Do not cite your own results or artifacts.

Task 7 — Prepare for Mentor Defense in the Final Project
Prepare a 15-minute presentation that covers:

Industry problem and motivation
System design and integration choices
Ethical considerations and safeguards
Evaluation results and limitations
Professional relevance and next steps
During the mentor meeting, you must defend your design choices, integration strategy, and ethical reasoning.

Task 8 — Final Review and Submission
Before submission, confirm that:

All required artifacts are included
The synthesis paper meets length and citation requirements
The system or case study clearly integrates at least three prior projects
Ethical reasoning is specific and grounded in your work
You are prepared to explain and defend your decisions
This project represents the culmination of the AI Mastery Capstone and must demonstrate system-level understanding, professional judgment, and responsible AI practice.

Overview
In this final synthesis project, you will design, integrate, and defend an industry-focused AI solution that demonstrates mastery across multiple domains of the capstone. You will select an industry or sector, define a real-world problem, and create a cohesive AI system, framework, or applied case study that integrates methods and insights from at least three prior projects. You will explain how earlier work informed your technical design choices, ethical reasoning, and professional decisions.

This project uses only the tools, techniques, and frameworks you have already used throughout the capstone. No new technologies are introduced. The emphasis is on synthesis, justification, and professional defense rather than implementation of novel methods.

You are producing an integrative, capstone-level artifact in this project. The focus is on system-level thinking, ethical integration, and the ability to clearly explain and defend design decisions in an industry context rather than building a fully deployed production system.

Why This Project Matters
Professional AI work requires the ability to integrate multiple components into a coherent, defensible solution. Employers and research stakeholders expect professionals to:

Synthesize data, modeling, generative, and agentic components into unified systems
Translate technical work into industry-relevant solutions
Justify design tradeoffs using evidence from prior analyses and implementations
Address ethical, governance, and societal considerations holistically
Communicate and defend decisions clearly to expert audiences
This project demonstrates readiness for advanced industry or research roles by connecting technical capability, ethical judgment, and professional communication into a single, coherent narrative.

What You Will Build
You will create:

An industry-specific AI solution or design artifact that:
addresses a clearly defined real-world problem
integrates methods or components from at least three prior capstone projects
demonstrates intentional system design and interaction between components
reflects realistic constraints, risks, and professional considerations
A reflective synthesis paper (1,500–2,000 words) that explains:
the industry context and problem definition
how prior projects informed system design and integration choices
technical and ethical tradeoffs encountered
limitations, risks, and professional relevance
A final mentor presentation and defense that:
presents the integrated system or case study
defends technical, ethical, and professional decisions
demonstrates authorship, understanding, and synthesis across domains
Citation Expectations
In your Integrative Industry Synthesis paper, you must use citations when you:

reference AI concepts, system design patterns, or evaluation frameworks
justify integration or architectural decisions
discuss ethical, governance, or responsible-use considerations
You do not need to cite your own prior project artifacts, code, or results. Your paper must end with a References section listing all sources used, such as course materials, textbooks, or official documentation.

Academic Integrity Expectations
This project is designed to verify authentic mastery and synthesis:

The integrated solution must be grounded in your own prior project work
Design reasoning must explicitly reference how earlier artifacts informed decisions
Ethical analysis must be specific to the chosen industry and system design
The mentor defense serves as confirmation of understanding, authorship, and professional readiness
Generic descriptions of AI systems or ethical considerations not tied to your integrated work will not meet project expectations.

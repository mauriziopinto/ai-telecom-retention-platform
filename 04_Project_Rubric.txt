Rubric
Use this project rubric to understand and assess the project criteria.

Technical Integration and Implementation
Criteria	Submission Requirements
Integrated AI System or Design Artifact

The submission includes a coherent AI system, framework, or applied case study that integrates methods or insights from at least three prior capstone projects. The artifact demonstrates intentional system design rather than a collection of disconnected components.

Cross-Domain Integration

The system clearly combines techniques from multiple AI domains such as data analysis, machine learning, deep learning, generative AI, or agentic systems. The integration is meaningful and supports the stated industry use case.

Execution or Conceptual Soundness

If implemented, the system runs as intended and produces observable outputs. If conceptual or design-focused, the artifact is technically plausible and grounded in methods covered in prior coursework.

Analytical Reasoning and Design Justification
Criteria	Submission Requirements
Industry Problem Definition

The chosen industry or sector is clearly defined, and the problem addressed is realistic, relevant, and appropriate for an AI-based solution.

Design Rationale

Design choices are clearly justified using the student’s prior project work. The student explains why specific models, workflows, or agentic patterns were selected and how earlier projects informed those decisions.

Tradeoffs and Constraints

The student identifies technical, data, or operational tradeoffs and explains constraints that shaped the final design.

Ethical and Responsible Practice
Criteria	Submission Requirements
Ethical Considerations

The synthesis paper identifies at least one ethical concern relevant to the proposed system, such as bias, misuse, transparency, accountability, or societal impact. The discussion is grounded in the specific industry context and system design.

Responsible Deployment Reasoning

The student explains safeguards, limitations, or governance considerations that would be required for responsible real-world use.

Communication and Professional Presentation
Criteria	Submission Requirements
Synthesis Paper Quality

The 1,500–2,000-word reflective synthesis paper is well organized, clearly written, and suitable for a professional or employer-facing audience. It explains integration rationale, technical decisions, ethical reasoning, and professional relevance.

Evidence-Based Reflection

Claims and conclusions in the paper are grounded in the student’s own prior projects, artifacts, and system design rather than generic descriptions.

Citations and References

In-text citations are used where required. A References section is included and follows a consistent citation style. At least two credible sources are cited, with at least one scholarly source.

Portfolio Integration and Professional Readiness
Criteria	Submission Requirements
Portfolio Value

The final artifact and paper together form a cohesive, capstone-level portfolio piece that demonstrates system-level thinking, cross-domain fluency, and industry relevance.

Documentation and Reproducibility

Supporting documentation is clear. If applicable, requirements.txt or configuration details are included so the system or analysis can be understood or reproduced.
